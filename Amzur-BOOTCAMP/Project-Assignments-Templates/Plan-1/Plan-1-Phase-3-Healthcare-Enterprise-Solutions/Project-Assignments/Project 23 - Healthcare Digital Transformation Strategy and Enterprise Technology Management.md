## **Testing Scenarios**

### **Strategic Leadership Challenges**

#### **Board-Level Technology Crisis**
**Scenario**: Major technology vendor relationship crisis requiring board-level intervention and strategic response
**Challenge**: Manage vendor crisis while maintaining technology operations and board confidence
**Expected Response**: Sophisticated crisis management with strategic vendor relationship resolution

#### **Digital Transformation Resistance**
**Scenario**: Significant organizational resistance to digital transformation from clinical and administrative staff
**Challenge**: Overcome resistance while accelerating transformation timeline and maintaining morale
**Expected Response**: Comprehensive change management with stakeholder engagement and resistance resolution

#### **Competitive Technology Threat**
**Scenario**: Major competitor launches breakthrough technology capability threatening market position
**Challenge**: Rapidly assess competitive threat and develop strategic technology response
**Expected Response**: Strategic competitive analysis with rapid technology evaluation and response plan

#### **Technology Investment Crisis**
**Scenario**: Economic downturn requiring 30% technology budget reduction while maintaining transformation goals
**Challenge**: Optimize technology portfolio and vendor relationships while continuing strategic progress
**Expected Response**: Strategic portfolio optimization with vendor renegotiation and priority realignment

### **Decision Points**

#### **Technology Strategy Philosophy**
- Innovation leadership vs. fast follower technology adoption approach
- Build vs. buy vs. partner strategies for different technology capabilities
- Cloud-first vs. hybrid vs. on-premise infrastructure strategy
- Vendor concentration vs. diversification for risk management

#### **Digital Transformation Approach**
- Big bang vs. phased transformation implementation
- Top-down vs. bottom-up change management strategy
- Centralized vs. decentralized technology governance
- Conservative vs. aggressive innovation and risk tolerance

#### **Organizational Change Strategy**
- Technology-driven vs. people-driven transformation approach
- Standardization vs. customization for different user groups
- Training-intensive vs. intuitive technology design emphasis
- Mandatory vs. voluntary technology adoption rollout
